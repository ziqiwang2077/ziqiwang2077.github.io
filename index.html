About Me

Graduate of a Master in Costume Design for Performance from the London College of Fashion (University of the Arts London), UK, and a Bachelor in Textile Design from Tsinghua University, China. Distinguished academic performer with a distinction in MA and a GPA of 3.61 in BA. Skilled in blending design theory with technological innovations, particularly in virtual reality and interactive costume. Demonstrated ability to execute research projects in haptic feedback and VR immersion, achieving first authorship in multiple international conference publications. Proficient in the development of multisensory design solutions that enhance user experience in virtual environments. Research interests include affective computing in VR, interactive haptic feedback systems, and the application of these technologies in educational settings.

Research Interest

I am deeply engaged in exploring how advanced technologies, particularly haptic feedback with both hardware and software, can transform virtual reality (VR) experiences. My research aims to develop systems that not only increase the sense of presence and immersion in VR but also adapt interactively to users' responses. This involves integrating visual and haptic feedback mechanisms , thereby enhancing the educational potential of VR environments.

    Interactive Installation Project

    The ECHO project focuses on the visualization of sound, drawing inspiration from the intricate structure of sound transmission and its relationship between individuals and the external world. The installation, worn by the performer, vividly demonstrates how invisible sound, such as echoes, interacts with and influences the human body.

    Fantasy World Design in VR--Alice Wonderland

    The Alice Wonderland project is designed for immersive VR theatre, exploring the lives of individuals with disabilities, such as hunchbacks and obesity. Users embark on a journey through a darker, surreal world, gaining insights into the challenges and experiences faced by these individuals.
